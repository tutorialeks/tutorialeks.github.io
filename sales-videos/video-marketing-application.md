## Instagram Reels Optimization (30-90 seconds)

**Instagram Reels algorithm favors**:
- Watch time (% of video watched)
- Replays
- Shares (especially to Stories/DMs)

### Visual Storytelling Tips

#### **Composition**

**Eye contact camera position**:
- For vulnerability: Direct eye contact (trust building)
- For teaching: Slightly off-camera (less intense)
- For B-roll: No face (illustrative)

**Framing**:
- Close-up for emotional moments (shows facial expressions)
- Medium shot for teaching/explanation
- Wide shot for scene-setting

#### **Lighting**

**Emotional scenes**: Softer, warmer light

**Consistency**: Keep lighting similar throughout for professional feel

## Production Guidelines

### Filming Tips for Authenticity

#### **Location Choices**

**For vulnerability scenes**:
- Personal space (your room, desk)
- Natural light
- Slightly messy (real life, not staged)

**For teaching scenes**:
- Clean background
- Professional but not sterile
- Maybe bookshelf or whiteboard visible

**For B-roll**:
- Real locations from story if possible
- TU Varna campus for exam stories
- Your actual workspace for thesis stories

---

#### **Camera Setup**

**DIY Setup** (Good enough):
- Smartphone on tripod
- Natural window light
- External mic (or phone close-up)

**Better Setup**:
- DSLR or mirrorless camera
- Ring light or softbox
- Lavalier mic

**Critical**: Stability > Quality. Shaky handheld is worse than lower-res stable.

---

#### **Performance Tips**

**For emotional delivery**:
- Actually remember the feeling
- Slow down (vulnerability requires pause)
- Eye contact with camera (builds trust)
- Don't "perform" - just tell

**For teaching delivery**:
- Steady pace (clear thinking)
- Slight smile (approachable)
- Gestures (emphasize points)
- Confident posture

---

### B-Roll Suggestions

**Use personal footage** for:

## Performance Metrics

### What to Track

**Engagement metrics**:
- **Views**: How many people saw it
- **Watch time %**: How many finished
- **Likes**: Basic engagement
- **Comments**: Deep engagement
- **Shares**: Viral potential
- **Saves**: High intent (reference later)

**Conversion metrics**:
- **Link clicks**: Bio link taps
- **Profile visits**: Interest in you
- **Follows**: Committed to your content
- **DMs**: Direct inquiry
- **Consultation bookings**: Actual leads

---

### Success Benchmarks

**Good performance**:
- 40%+ completion rate
- 5%+ like rate (likes/views)
- 2%+ comment rate
- 3%+ save rate

**Great performance**:
- 60%+ completion rate
- 8%+ like rate
- 4%+ comment rate
- 5%+ save rate

**Viral performance**:
- 80%+ completion rate
- Shares > comments
- Exponential view growth over 48 hours

---

### What to Test (A/B Testing Ideas)

**Hook variations**:
- Test 3 different hooks for same story
- See which gets highest retention

**Length testing**:
- 45s vs. 60s vs. 90s
- Find sweet spot for your audience

**CTA testing**:
- Soft vs. hard CTA
- Placement (mid-video vs. end)

**Story type testing**:
- Exam stories vs. coursework stories
- Personal vs. student success stories

**Posting time**:
- Morning vs. evening
- Weekday vs. weekend

---

### Iteration Strategy

**The 3-video rule**:
- Don't judge performance on 1 video
- Post at least 3 with similar format
- Then analyze trends

**Double down on winners**:
- If exam stories perform better, make more
- If 60s length works best, stick with it
- If vulnerability hooks win, emphasize them

**Kill the losers**:
- If hard CTAs scare people off, soften
- If 90s videos have poor retention, shorten
- If teaching-only videos bore, add more story
